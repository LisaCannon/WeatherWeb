Summary: Latitude vs X

Is latitudinal location an indicator of certain weather conditions? Locations close to the equater, Latitude are thought to be hotter. Tropical vacation destinations come to mind, suggesting maybe locations closer to the equator are more cloudy and humid. The purpose of this study is to determine whether Latitude has a relationship with weather conditions. Weather conditions investigated here include temperaure, cloudiness, humidity, and wind speed.


To answer these questions, weather data from over 500 cities around the globe was pulled from the OpenWeatherMap API using randomly generated coordinates. These data observations are availble in the data link.

The results of the study suggest that there is a strong quadratic relationship between temperature and latitude. Temperatures are hotter near the center of the globe and cooler towards the poles. The peak of this trend is not at the equator, as predicted, but instead about 15° north of the equator.

There was no detectible relationship with latitude and cloudiness, humidity, or wind speed. Those weather conditions seam to be uniformly distributed across at measures of latitude.
